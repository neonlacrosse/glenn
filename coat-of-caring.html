A Poem

A Coat of Caring

A new song not yet sung

The air was cold and snow deep.
Restless seekers came in to eat.
The shelter opened at seven.
For food and a warm place to meet.
She entered with the crowd.
Appearing just shy of despair,
from falling on hard times,
at the intersection of going nowhere.
Chorus
She said she was weary and cold.
Worn well beyond her years.
Years of self doubt and tears.
A volunteer came to say
I can help with being cold.
I have a coat with me today.
My wife said give it away.
The coat, a color of dusty rose,
Impeccable in design
Of finest wool composed,
She said it's too fine for me.
Chorus
She said she was weary and cold.
Worn well beyond her years.
Years of self doubt and tears.
She took a chance, putting the coat on.
Her darkness fell away to the floor.
Enveloped in luxury, soft and warm.
I've not had anything this fine before.
It was a coat of caring, given in love,
To lift her self image and self worth.
It came as a message, from above.
A chance to start over, a rebirth.
It was a coat helping her to care.
Care for herself in her time ahead.
Lifting away the dark clouds of despair.
It was a coat of caring.
— A coat of caring —
